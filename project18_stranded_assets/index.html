Stranded Assets Valuation Under Climate Scenarios

Monte Carlo Simulation of Fossil Fuel Asset Impairment

Languages: Python & R Category: Sustainable Finance Analytics
Research Question What is the expected financial loss on fossil fuel assets under different NGFS climate transition scenarios?
Methodology

Monte Carlo simulation modelling the probability distribution of fossil fuel asset values under three NGFS scenarios (Hot House, Middle of the Road, Net Zero 2050). Incorporates carbon price paths, demand curves, and regulatory timeline uncertainty.

Data Collection & Sources
Framework: NGFS climate scenarios (Phase IV)
Scenarios: Hot House World, Middle of the Road, Net Zero 2050
Assets: Simulated oil, gas, and coal reserve portfolios
Parameters: Carbon prices, demand elasticities, regulatory timelines, discount rates
Key Methods
Monte Carlo Simulation
10,000 scenario draws per asset — samples from probability distributions of carbon prices, demand, and regulation timing
NGFS Scenario Framework
Three standardised pathways: Hot House (no policy), Middle Road (gradual), Net Zero (aggressive) — provides structured uncertainty
Value-at-Risk (VaR)
95th percentile loss estimate for fossil fuel portfolio under each scenario — translates climate risk into financial risk metric
Key Findings
1
Under Net Zero 2050, expected fossil fuel asset impairment is 45–65% of current book value (median Monte Carlo estimate)
2
Coal assets face near-total stranding (80–95%) even under Middle of the Road scenario due to cheaper renewable alternatives
3
Oil & gas show high variance — outcome depends critically on CCS adoption rate and carbon price trajectory timing

import numpy as np
